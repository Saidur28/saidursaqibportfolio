Prophets in Islam

Islam recognizes many prophets sent by Allah to guide humanity. The Quran mentions 25 prophets by name, each chosen to deliver Allah's message to their people.

List of 25 Prophets Mentioned in the Quran

  1. Prophet Adam (A.S): The first human and prophet, the father of humanity.
  2. Prophet Idris (A.S): Known for his wisdom and knowledge.
  3. Prophet Nuh (A.S): Preached for 950 years and built the Ark.
  4. Prophet Hud (A.S): Warned the people of Ad who rejected Allah.
  5. Prophet Salih (A.S): Sent to the people of Thamud with the she-camel as a sign.
  6. Prophet Ibrahim (A.S): The "Father of Monotheism," known for his unwavering faith.
  7. Prophet Ismail (A.S): Helped Ibrahim build the Kaaba and demonstrated patience.
  8. Prophet Ishaq (A.S): Blessed with prophethood and the continuation of Ibrahim's message.
  9. Prophet Yaqub (A.S): Father of the 12 tribes of Israel.
  10. Prophet Yusuf (A.S): Known for his beauty, wisdom, and story of patience.
  11. Prophet Shuï¿½ayb (A.S): Preached to the people of Madyan about honesty in trade.
  12. Prophet Musa (A.S): Led the Israelites and was given the Torah.
  13. Prophet Harun (A.S): Supported Musa in delivering Allah's message.
  14. Prophet Dhul-Kifl (A.S): Known for his patience and steadfastness.
  15. Prophet Dawud (A.S): King and prophet, given the Psalms (Zabur).
  16. Prophet Sulaiman (A.S): Ruled with wisdom and controlled jinn and animals.
  17. Prophet Ilyas (A.S): Called his people to abandon idol worship.
  18. Prophet Al-Yasa (A.S): Continued the mission of Ilyas.
  19. Prophet Yunus (A.S): Swallowed by a whale after leaving his people but was forgiven after repentance.
  20. Prophet Zakariya (A.S): Prayed for a child and was blessed with Yahya.
  21. Prophet Yahya (A.S): Known for his purity and righteousness.
  22. Prophet Isa (A.S): Born miraculously and given the Gospel (Injeel).
  23. Prophet Lut (A.S): Preached against immoral behavior to the people of Sodom.
  24. Prophet Ayyub (A.S): A model of patience during trials and hardship.
  25. Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W): The final prophet and the seal of all prophets, sent as mercy to all of humanity.

Significance of the Prophets

The prophets (peace be upon them) taught monotheism, morality, and worship of Allah. Their stories in the Quran provide timeless lessons of faith, patience, and perseverance.

"And We sent not before you any messenger except that We revealed to him that, 'There is no deity except Me, so worship Me.'" - Surah Al-Anbiya (21:25)
