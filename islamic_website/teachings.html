The Teachings of Islam

Islam is a comprehensive way of life that emphasizes peace, submission to Allah, and the betterment of society. At its heart are the Five Pillars of Islam, which serve as the foundation of a Muslim's faith and practice.

The Five Pillars of Islam are the core teachings that guide a Muslim's life.

The Five Pillars of Islam

  1. Shahada (Faith): The declaration of faith, "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."
  2. Salah (Prayer): Performing the five daily prayers at designated times to maintain a connection with Allah.
  3. Zakat (Charity): Giving a portion of wealth (2.5%) to help the poor and those in need, purifying one's earnings and soul.
  4. Sawm (Fasting): Observing fasting during the month of Ramadan as an act of worship, self-control, and gratitude.
  5. Hajj (Pilgrimage): Performing the pilgrimage to the holy city of Makkah at least once in a lifetime, for those who are physically and financially able.

The Importance of the Five Pillars

The Five Pillars serve as a framework for a Muslim's spiritual and moral life. They encourage discipline, unity, and compassion, helping individuals strengthen their relationship with Allah and serve humanity.

"And establish prayer and give zakah and bow with those who bow [in worship and obedience]." - Surah Al-Baqarah (2:43)
